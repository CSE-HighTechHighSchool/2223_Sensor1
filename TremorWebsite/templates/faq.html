Frequently Asked Questions

Click on a question for the answer

What is the TremorTrackr?

The TremorTracker is a wearable device—composed of a comfortable sweatband and a 3D-printed box housing three different sensors (including an IMU sensor, EMG sensor, and Arduino Nano)—that allows users to track their tremors. This device works with an easy-to-use website to help individuals track their data and easily share it with their doctor. Our goal is to help individuals reduce tremor frequency and severity as much as possible to help them live a more comfortable life.

How do I sign up?

Go to the “Sign In” page on our website. Then, register with TremorTracker by following the directions on the screen. When you are ready to sign in, you will just need to use your email and password.

How do I use the TremorTracker?

See the“Procedure” page on our website for detailed steps on how to use the TremorTracker!

Do I need to pay to use the website?

No, the website is completely free to use!

How long can I wear the TremorTracker for?

You can wear the device for as long as you would like! However, once you are done collecting data, feel free to take it off.

What information will the website provide me with?

The website will provide you with data on muscle activity, including the linear acceleration and angular velocity of your tremors. You will also be able to see accompanying graphs and charts.

Does it matter which color electrode I place where?

No, the collected data will remain the same no matter which color electrode is placed at each location on the forearm.

Do I need to place all three electrodes on my arm?

Yes, all three electrodes are needed to ensure that the most accurate data is obtained. This will help your doctor better understand your tremors and determine the proper course of action moving forward to help you better manage your tremors.

Does it matter which arm I use to record data?

No, you can choose to use either your left or right arm for collecting data.

Can I record tremors in both of my arms at the same time?

No, you can only collect data for one arm at a time.
